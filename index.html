Frequently Asked Questions

When is the UPSC Prelims 2026 exam being held?+
The UPSC Civil Services Preliminary Examination 2026 is scheduled for 24 May 2026 (Sunday). Paper 1 (General Studies) runs 09:30 to 11:30 IST and Paper 2 (CSAT) runs 14:30 to 16:30 IST.
Are Set A, B, C and D answer keys different?+
No. The question paper content is identical across the four sets — UPSC only shuffles the order of questions. Pick your set in the answer-key controls above and the table reorders to match the paper you wrote.
How is the UPSC Prelims 2026 score calculated?+
Each correct GS Paper 1 answer is worth 2 marks. Each wrong answer deducts 0.66 marks (one-third of 2). Unattempted questions carry no penalty. Use the score calculator above to estimate your total.
What is the expected cutoff for UPSC Prelims 2026?+
Based on the trailing 5-year average and difficulty signals, the General category cutoff for 2026 is expected to land in the 80–90 band out of 200. The expected-cutoff table above breaks this down by category.
Is CSAT counted towards the Prelims cutoff?+
No. CSAT (Paper 2) is qualifying only — you must score 33% (66 of 200 marks) to qualify, but the cutoff for moving to Mains is decided entirely by GS Paper 1 marks.
How can I challenge an answer in the official UPSC answer key?+
After the official provisional key drops on the UPSC website, you can submit objections through their grievance portal within the stipulated window — typically 7 days. Include the question paper set, question number and supporting references.
What is the CSAT qualifying score?+
The CSAT qualifying threshold is 66 out of 200 marks (33%). Candidates who score below this threshold are disqualified regardless of their GS Paper 1 score.
